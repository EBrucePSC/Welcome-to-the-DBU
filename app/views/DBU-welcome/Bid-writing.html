DBU bid writing

Many of the projects we work on in the DBU are first advertised on Digital Marketplace. If you're assigned to the DBU, you may be asked to get involved with writing bids for this work.

There are a number of steps to writing, and winning, a bid. This page gives an overview of the key bid-writing stages, and includes links to resources that will help you improve your writing skills.

Writing 100 word answers

For projects advertised on Digital Marketplace, the first stage of the application involves submitting a series of 100-word respones to demonstrate that The PSC meets the 'essential' and 'nice-to-have' skills and experience required to deliver the work.

Each answer is scored out of 3, depending on whether requirements are not met, met, or exceeded. The top scoring suppliers will be shortlisted, and invited to submit a written proposal and/or pitch for the work.

Steps to writing a good 100 word answer
  1. Read through the tender, including the ‘essential’ and ‘nice to have’ criteria. Can we meet these requirements already, or would we need to find a partner?
  2. Copy the 100 word answer master template into the project folder on google. Populate with the ‘essential’ and ‘nice to have’ questions
  3. Identify the most relevant example for each question
    1. Try and use the most relevant example for the 1st question (ideally a case study with similar subject matter and the same / a similar client)
    2. Aim for a mix of case studies that demonstrate breadth and depth of experience
    3. If you’re not sure where to start, search for key terms in our 100 word answers database. This will help you identify case studies we've used for similar case studies before
  4. Follow the steps on the spreadsheet - either pasting in old answer and iterate until it addresses the question, or write a new answer from scratch. Each answer should explain:
    1. What we did
    2. How we did it
    3. What result this achieved (add quantitative stats if possible)
  5. Follow The PSC's bid writing style throughout.
